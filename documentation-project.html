Artist Statement
The Artist explains their works and thought-processes that went into making them.
Vision Videos

  • Inspired by the traditional art form of stained glass windows, Vision Videos recognizes the beauty of sun-catching transparent art, while also forcing the viewer to question the normality of traditional materials to facilitate a shifted focus towards new material innovations. Framed in a reused CD case, and made entirely of layered kombucha SCOBY leather, Vision Videos was created with the future of sustainability in mind. The sentimental nature of the bygone technology of CDs juxtaposed with the forward-thinking living material that grows itself, leads to an interesting balance between past and future. The imagery of a landscape reminds us to stay grounded in the present in the midst of two conflicting innovations, moving apart in time otherwise. Unlike stained glass, the unique properties of the SCOBY leather allow pieces to be layered, creating varying values of transparency, and drawing depth to desired areas.

    • Sensual Crane I & II

  • Sensual Cranes I & II are pre-cursor projects used to gain familiarity with the Kombucha SCOBY leather material qualities. The first part of the name, Sensual, comes not from the common correlation of the word (with sex), but by the literal meaning of it - something that invokes all the senses. Working with kombucha SCOBY to make a leather called all my senses to action - taste, smell, touch, and sight (I was trying to think of how it could have invoked the sense of hearing, but admittedly that would have been a stretch). The SCOBY has a vinegary taste, with a slimy mouth feel and chewy texture like a gummy. When dehydrated, it has the same texture as fruit leather and tastes like a more concentrated vinegary and somewhat sweet gummy. When feeling the SCOBY pre-dehydration it has a very rubbery-smooth, wet texture. Once dehydrated it feels very similar to leather or skin. The leather has visible traits of transparency and a slightly veiny, speckled texture. The smell of the leather filled my house with an apple cider vinegar aroma for almost the whole 5 hours that It took to dehydrate in the oven. When the leather came out of the oven and when I was working with the material to make art, it left a sticky residue on my fingers and would stick to itself if left too long (probably due to the humidity in the air slightly rehydrating it). Cranes - I was inspired to make cranes as my first experiment because I grew up as an origami kid. A story my friend in school had told me really stuck and I’ve held with me since - the story of Sadako and 1000 cranes. In the story it says whoever folds 1000 cranes will be granted one wish. Ever since this, I’ve always folded paper cranes out of random scraps around me whenever I needed something to fidget with in my hands. Whether it was gum wrappers or napkins, I had the paper crane pattern memorized and was always ready to habitually fold. This impromptu origami experiment of Sensual Cranes was also sparked by a failure in my original plans when making the SCOBY leather. After dehydration, the leather came out more brittle and fragile than I had expected. I went into this project hoping to make a SCOBY leather clutch or wallet with the amount of material I had planned for. I had hoped for one solid sheet of the leather to peel right off of the pan, but instead it was a struggle peeling bit by bit from the aluminum it clung to. I managed to save a few big pieces of the leather, but they were abnormally shaped. Feeling defeated and like my project was a bust, I was struggling with coming up with a new idea on how to make do with what I had. Naturally my brain went to the innate resourcefulness that I had been using as subconscious crutch for my whole childhood. I saw I could make a few squares just big enough from my scraps to fold the origami cranes that I had crafted so many times before, this time with a new, unfamiliar material that would take more intentional focus than my normal crane folding routines.

    • Ideation
    The Idea behind the projects.

    I was motivated to do this project because I plan on researching sustainable material ecology and textile design post-graduation. I had been looking at diy crafts to dip my toes into the sustainable material design realm. There are many easy projects to conduct such as a bio foam that I made with agar agar powder and bicarbonate, but kombucha leather is something that has always been on my mind since researching the topic. I started a Kombucha SCOBY back in May of 2020 during my quarantine from COVID, and have kept it alive and nurtured it since - for over 2 years now. Seeing that I could make a sustainable material with something I had already been making for years opened a whole new passion in my mind. I began my research of artist who had already made kombucha leather and what their projects were. Most made wearables, so I wanted to make a wearable as well to truly show off the fabric-substitution properties of the material.

    • Influences & Inspiration
    The sparks of Inspiration and the Influential people who motivated the projects.

    My passion started with learning abut Neri Oxman and her work with MIT Media Lab. I felt we were truly on the same wavelength and that her passions aligned with my passions, the only difference was that she was making actionable change. Her research about material design and the new field of study she Invented Calle Material Ecology led me down a rabbit hole of Inspiration. I started looking for programs I could be Involved In to further my education In the field. I stumbled upon a 6 month program In Barcelona, Spain called fabricademy from IAAC, where all the courses spoke directly to me. I further researched what course wok looked like and came across a giant open source library of student projects, and most Inspiring - step by step guides on how to replicate the arterials they were making.
